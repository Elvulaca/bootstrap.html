Step 1:

  • Dice the goat meat
  • Chop the garlic, onions and ginger
  • Prepare your herbs and spices: curry powder, tumeric powder, cumin seeds, mixed spice powder, mustard seeds, fresh curry leaves, fresh bay leaves, coriander and cinnamon sticks
  • Coat the meat in salt and pepper and set aside until ready to cook

Step 2:

  1. Sautee garlic, onions and ginger with the spices and curry and bay leaves
  2. Mix curry powder and mixed spice with a cup of water and add to the wok
  3. Stir in the meat, add cinnamon sticks and cover to cook for 25mins, stirring periodically
  4. Add a tablespoon of plain yoghurt and stir
  5. Add the coriander leaves and cover wok again to let the meat cook

Step 3: Making the roti

  1. Prepare sharp flour, boiled water, salt, a rolling pin, and ghee
  2. Boil the water in a kettle or pot, while waiting to boil, pour sharp flour in a bowl and add a pinch of salt
  3. When water is ready, add to the flour and mix with a wooden spoon until it reaches a dough consistency
  4. When comfortable to do so, use your hands to gently knead the dough and add two tbsp of ghee to keep the dough soft
  5. Break off pieces of dough and roll each one into a ball
  6. When ready to roll your roti, take a dough ball and flatten it gently and coat both sides with flour to be easier to roll
  7. Using the rolling pin, roll out the dough flat into circles, like a tortilla
  8. Place the rolled out roti onto a preheated pan and commence cooking, flipping on both sides for an even cook. NOTE: the first two flips, you should dab ghee on the roti focusing on the edges so they do not form a crispy edge
